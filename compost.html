What is Composting?

Composting is nature’s recycling system. It transforms organic waste like food scraps and garden trimmings into nutrient-rich soil that improves plant growth and supports a healthy ecosystem.

Why Should We Compost?

Composting diverts waste from landfills, reduces methane emissions, and returns vital nutrients to the soil. It’s a small step that makes a big difference for both your garden and the planet.

How Does Composting Work?

Microorganisms, oxygen, and moisture break down organic matter into humus. A dark, crumbly material that enriches soil. Balance greens (like food scraps) with browns (like leaves or cardboard) for the best results.

The circle of life!

What Can Be Composted?

  • ✔️ Fruit and vegetable peels
  • ✔️ Coffee grounds and tea bags
  • ✔️ Dried leaves, twigs, and cardboard
  • ✔️ Eggshells and paper scraps

Tips for Healthy Compost

Keep your compost moist but not soggy, turn it regularly for aeration, and maintain a 3:1 ratio of browns to greens. Patience is key, nature takes time to create something valuable.
